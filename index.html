An introduction to astronomy

Astronomy comes from the Greek words astro- (άστρο) meaning star and -nomy (νομία) meaning to study something, and it is the branch of science that studies celestial objects, space, and the universe as a whole. Some branches of astronomy include cosmology (which studies the origins of our universe and its future), astrophysics (studying the physical properties of celestial objects), and many others (astrorradiology, astrochemistry, astobiology just to name a few). People have been studying the night sky since around 1000 BE, and astronomy really gained popularity at the enlightenment era alongside many other sciences. Some famous astronomers are:

  • Galileo Galilei
  • Johannes Kepler
  • Nicolaus Copernicus
  • Edwin Hubble
  • Nancy Roman
  • Neil deGrasse Tyson
